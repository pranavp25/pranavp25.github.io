## Commitment to Mentorship and Outreach

Beyond my research, I am committed to mentorship, teaching, and fostering an inclusive scientific community. As a Teaching Assistant for multiple undergraduate physics courses at UC Irvine, I strive to create a supportive, student-centered learning environment that encourages curiosity, critical thinking, and confidence in physics.

Mentorship is an important part of my work. Through the Physics and Astronomy Community Excellence (PACE) program, I mentor incoming graduate students as they transition into graduate school and advise undergraduate students applying to REU programs and graduate school. 

I have also co-advised two undergraduate honors thesis projects alongside my faculty advisor, mentoring students in exoplanet research, scientific programming, data analysis, and scientific communication as they completed their senior thesis research.

I am an active participant in the Department of Physics and Astronomy's outreach initiatives, engaging with K–12 students and the broader community through public outreach events that promote STEM education and inspire the next generation of scientists.

Within the department, I have also been involved in several initiatives focused on graduate student support and community building. I previously served as an administrator for the Physics Graduate Caucus (PGC), where I advocated for graduate student interests, worked to strengthen communication between students and departmental leadership, supported Teaching Assistants through resource development and peer mentoring, and organized initiatives that fostered a stronger sense of community within the graduate program.

Through these experiences, I have developed a lasting commitment to creating an academic environment where students feel supported, valued, and empowered to succeed while helping build a more welcoming and inclusive physics and astronomy community.
