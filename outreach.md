## Commitment to Mentorship and Outreach

Beyond my research, I am committed to mentorship, teaching, and fostering an inclusive scientific community. As a Teaching Assistant for multiple undergraduate physics courses at UC Irvine, I strive to create a supportive, student-centered learning environment that encourages curiosity and confidence in physics.

I am an active participant in the Department of Physics and Astronomy's outreach initiatives, engaging with K–12 students and the broader community through public outreach events that promote STEM education and inspire the next generation of scientists.

Within the department, I have been involved in several initiatives focused on graduate student support and community building. As a mentor in the Physics and Astronomy Community Excellence (PACE) program, I have helped first-year graduate students navigate the transition into graduate school while also advising undergraduate students applying to REU programs and graduate school.

I previously served as an administrator for the Physics Graduate Caucus (PGC), where I advocated for graduate student interests, worked to strengthen communication between students and departmental leadership, supported Teaching Assistants through resource development and peer support, and organized initiatives that fostered a stronger sense of community within the graduate program. Through these experiences, I have developed a lasting commitment to improving the academic environment, supporting my peers, and making physics and astronomy more welcoming and accessible for all members of the community.
